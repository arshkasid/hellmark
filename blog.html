Life at L

Campus Life

YOHO - You only Hel(L) once!

IIM Lucknow, spread over 185 acres, is a world in itself with a residential facility for 1000+ students and faculty, departmental stores which stock everything from pins to bins, medical and stationery stores.

The red buildings, well-maintained lawns, and the statue of Medusa add to the beauty of the campus.

So how does a typical day look like at IIM Lucknow, also popularly referred to Hel(L)? There is no single day that will look alike as Hell has a lot of surprise elements in store. The schedule here is rigorous. Sleep? You will have to make a trade-off between sleep and parties and assignments. There will be a lot of quizzes, both announced and surprise quizzes, followed by assignments, projects, presentations, and guest lectures lined up one after the other.

Blissful Mornings (Part 1 of the day)


Walking up to the sounds of peacocks, you can see a lot of people jogging/walking when there is no 8 am quiz. Most of the days, it is just the students rushing to their morning class from mess or straight from hostel rooms. There are usually around 4-5 hours of lectures every day which sometimes happen till 6 pm.

Pleasant Evenings (Part 2 of the day)


Evenings are so lively at Hell. The campus offers various choices for you to spend your evening. You can choose to spend it in sports grounds, go for an evening walk or meet your group members to discuss your projects. A lot of Clubs, Committees, and Academic Interest groups (aka CCAs) organize their competitions, speaker sessions, and fun events.

Demanding Night Life (Part 3 of the day)


Insti-parties (Institute parties) keep happening now and then, to add more colour to your rigorous academic life. The beats of the DJ let you dance against the rhythms of boring life. Other times, students usually gossip among friends, get ready for birthday bumps, and even prepare for the next day’s class or quizzes. Most of them visit night mess (operates from 11 pm to 3 am) to share and lament how their day went with scrumptious food. No matter how happy, sad, or tired you are, the night mess food does its magic.

Dance and art workshops, the DJ nights, Sangarsh, Camaraderie, and various other events come to rescue you from the over-demanding academic schedule. Sundays are always uncertain. Not every Sunday is an off day if you are a Hel(L)’ite and not to forget deadlines are sacrosanct.

Hostel Life at L

Home away from Home!

When the first-year students enter the IIM Lucknow campus, they have a prevalent misconception that HelL is all about work and no play. On the contrary, the hostel life at HelL helps the students balance the academic rigor and their packed schedules. Instead, it would be full of fun and happy memories that the students could cherish later on.

IIM Lucknow is set amidst a sprawling area of 200 acres, and it is a fully residential institute housing both the faculties and the students. There are 17 student hostels on the campus (a.k.a H1 to H17). The hostels have single-occupancy rooms, which are spacious, and the rooms have a bed, a closet, and a study table. Each room has two big-screened doors, a window, and a small balcony. In addition to this, each hostel room is provided with intranet and Wi-Fi facilities to make the students' lives easier and connected.

Hostel blocks


Each hostel has its perks. For instance, if you are in a hostel from H1 to H8, you are fairly close to the mess and the academic blocks. Hence you do not have to skip your meals, and the fun fact is that you can start just 5 minutes before the class to be on time. Whereas, if you are in Hostel H15 or H16, which is more than a kilometer away from the academic blocks, then attending the early morning 9 am classes is a challenge (if you're a late night owl). But Hostel H16 is dubbed as the party hostel, and the late-night fun on the campus happens here. Be it the insti-parties or the inauguration of the camaraderie (inter-section wars); everything happens at H16. There are other prime locations like the Titanic over H10, H15, common areas and connectors that connect between adjacent hostels. The fun does not stop here, and even some enthusiastic hosts (of course, they'll be your fellow batchmates) organize parties in their room. In short, the nightlife at hostels H1 to H8 is entirely in contrast to that of hostels H9 to H16.

Facilities in & around the Hostels


There is a wide range of sports facilities at IIML for both outdoor and indoor games. To name a few, the hostels have facilities for badminton courts, table tennis, and carrom. The students can access them 24/7.

Kudos to the Mess Committee (Trivia - IIM L mess is run solely by the students) for installing vending machines at different parts of the hostels and the academic blocks. This aids the students in pulling off frequent all-nighters (be it parties or exams) with ease. Besides this, the night mess also delivers food to hostels so that it would make the life of students easier in hostels farther from the mess. Moreover, the food outlets within the campus have some exciting menus that the students would not be able to resist. Although the IIM Lucknow campus is located at a distance of about 8 km on the city's outskirts, the students can enjoy the liberty of ordering from restaurants like Dominos and KFC.

The campus also houses a well-stocked mini departmental store run by Kendriya Bhandar, Post Office, Bank, and a Health Centre. The library at IIM Lucknow is a spacious 30,000 sq. ft. two-storied building. The students can access this facility 24 x 7 to enrich themselves.

Initially, life as a HelL’ite might seem like a daunting challenge, but once you enter the campus and go through all the experiences with your peers, you would reminisce and miss them when you leave the campus.

Language of L

Much of MBA, put simply, is about adding jargon to common sense. For a freshman embarking upon the B-school journey, it is essential to acquaint himself with the campus lingo, which otherwise might sound like Greek. Like Hogwarts’ “Expecto Patronum”, IIM Lucknow has a plethora of colloquial jargons. Here are the most common ones:



1. Hel(L):


Any idea – why IIM L is called Hel(L)???.... Given its academic rigor, IIM Lucknow is popularly called HelL, but believe me for a fact, by the time you graduate, the only word that you’d use to describe this place would be “heaven”. HelL eventually turns out to be a misnomer amidst the immense opportunities that the campus offers. The inmates of HelL are called: HelLites, HelLions, HelL-Riders, and so on.

2. Globe:



Globe refers to bombastic verbiage laced with fancy business faff, usually making less or no sense. Despite being overrated, it is one essential skill to have to survive at HelL. Among the various skills that MBA teaches you, ‘Globe’ing is one of them. The one who globes frequently is called a Globe-Master.

3. BG-1 & BG-2:


BG-1 and BG-2, perhaps the place where you would end up spending maximum time and making the best memories, stands for Bodhi Grih-1 and Bodhi Grih-2. They are the academic blocks that have all classrooms and exam halls.

4. DCP:



DCP or Desperate Class Participation is an act of overtly and desperately putting forth one’s opinion during a class discussion with the sole motive of gaining some extra class participation marks. Another three-lettered word very similar to DCP is ACP or After Class Participation (self-explanatory).

5. Free-Rider:



Free-Rider is an adjective used to describe a person who makes less or no contribution to a group project, more so without any guilt of reaping the fruits of others’ efforts. All project groups, with no exception, have a free rider.

6. Fauji:



A picturesque spot-on campus with a green meadow on one side and general stores on the other. Fondly called, ‘Fauji area’ where musical gatherings happen occasionally.

7. Lite-lo:



You’ll hear this very frequently. “Lite-le” would generally mean “don’t take stress”, but this is a fairly versatile term and has various other usages too. For example: “Is plan ko Lite-lo” would mean let's drop the plan, “Ye lite course hai” would mean “We will sail through this course”.

8. Medusa:


Another campus spot with a gigantic statue resembling Medusa. It is the place for some deep moment of soul searching or to enjoy a moment of solace. In fact, the IIM L store – HellMark’s logo traces its origin from the Medusa statue on the campus.

9. CC:


CC or the Computer Centre will be your go-to place for discussing group projects, taking print-outs or for simply chilling in AC.

10. Titanic:



For all the party animals, the Titanic is the destination. The coolest party place on the campus. Wondering why it is called Titanic? Well, that’s for you to discover.

Things to do before entering L

Finally belled the CAT, then made through rounds of WAT & PI. That was one HelL of a journey indeed. Wasn’t that? And roughly 2 months before you sink yourself in the realm of the legendary campus that is fondly called Hel(L). Counting days to it? Well, that shall come soon too.

The red buildings, well-maintained lawns, and the statue of Medusa add to the beauty of the campus.

This is the phase of new aspirations, anxiety, happiness & lots of wondering & pondering over what to do, how to prepare oneself, and how to be the best among the best. The period before an MBA is a great time for reflection and mentally preparing yourself to potentially reinvent yourself. To help you out, the IIM L Store – HellMark has prepared a list of To-Do things before we welcome you here.

1. Learn Excel & Powerpoint, No Excuses!



Excel & Powerpoint may seem to be a piece of cake but don’t undermine the proficiency needed for any management graduate. Irrespective of your background, it will be helpful for you to learn them because, to put it simply, they will be the bread and butter for your next 2 years. Go skills website (https://www.goskills.com/) has a free trial for the first 7 days. Complete at least a couple of courses at this time. “Excel Advanced” is a must. Also, you can check out Udemy (https://www.udemy.com/). They have very reasonably priced courses too.

2. Of ‘the holy’ CV & Resume



These 2 months will be the perfect time to recollect what you did & achieved in life and get the first draft of your CV ready. Start collecting all the points you can think of, big or small, relevant or irrelevant. Once you’re on campus, you will be inundated with academic rigor, committee selections, and before you realize anything, you will be sitting for your summers. So, buckle up, buoy!

3. Math & Finance Anxiety? Shoo them away!



As most of you must be aware, the first year of MBA does no discrimination based on your previous Karma. Engineers will have to go through the finance courses in all three terms, and Commerce grads have no relief with heavy Quant courses in all three terms. So, what to do? It will be a good idea to have a basic math preparation & get comfy with finance & accounting terms. Engineers, we feel you! (Accounting basics in Udemy) (Basics in Business Finance) Commerce grads, hey there! Every year before first-year students join the program, IIM L would offer a math preparatory course of its own. Do try to enroll for that if you feel it’s needed.

4. One more Presentation? Interviews, Oh no!



Be it presentations, networking, or company selections, Soft skills will always be the icing on the cake of your technical know-how. So, if you’re good, become better and if you’re not good, but this time to use.

Vibrant Life at L

You’ll experience a vibrant life at L encompassed by various activities happening throughout the year. Here, you may get the glimpse of a few such activities….

Clubs & Committees:


Name it and you have it, IIM Lucknow has over a score of student bodies-Clubs, Committees, Academic Interest Groups and Student Ventures. Whatever your passion be, a subject, an art or a skill; you will easily find a related student body with like-minded people. And the best thing about them is, you get a close-knitted group of people that become a family away from home.

Insti-Parties and Festivals:


If you thought MBA was just about the 3Ps(Padhai, Placements and PPTs), well, you were wrong. The IIM L fraternity celebrates every festival, every occasion, every event with full fervour. Insti-parties organised at the campus are crazy fun. That is where you switch from networking to some real bonding.

Manfest-Varchasva:


IIM Lucknow hosts two mega-scale fests- Manfest Varchasva and INDEX. The only time you will get to meet your friends from other B-Schools in Campus would be during these. Besides, getting to organize these fests will be a learning in itself.

Live Projects and Case Competitions:


The real hands-on learning, take it for every word, happens through case competitions and live projects from companies, corporates and clubs. Each time you do a case competition your understanding of the concerned sector becomes crystal clear from head to heel. A case could be something as big as planning a Kumbh Mela, planning the launch of a brand or running a live campaign.

Section Wars:


Camaraderie the inter-section war, where all 9 sections fight for the ultimate trophy, fosters fevicol bond among the inmates of every section, a bond that lasts forever. Three days of crazy fun-dancing, singing, painting, eating, playing, spamming and what not. By hook or by crook, every section fights to its full might. Sangram, the sports war is another big inter-section event.

Birthday Celebrations:


HelLites are magnanimous when it comes to birthdays; we smear one whole cake on the person’s face on birthdays. Thinking of GPLs? Don’t worry, you will have a good mix of GPL, eggs, cold water, cough syrup, and some secret ingredients (read surprise quizzes) hurled at you on birthdays.

Night Mess:



The Annapurna Night mess, which runs till 3:00 am on regular days and till 4:00 am during exams, is absolute bliss. After a long and tiring day, a cup of cold coffee, a plate of Aloo paratha (laced with butter), and a fresh serving of pav bhaji heals the soul.

P.S: They serve Non-Veg too (Galouti Kebab is bae).

Prom-Night:


Ladies and gentlemen, pack the best of your apparel. Prom-Night (who needs an explanation) is one hot-shot event to look up to. Wondering “Mera number kab aaega?”, lo aa gaya.

TEDx:


Yes, you read it right. IIM Lucknow hosts TEDx on campus. So those fancy Facebook DP filters that you had always fancied will now be on your DP.
